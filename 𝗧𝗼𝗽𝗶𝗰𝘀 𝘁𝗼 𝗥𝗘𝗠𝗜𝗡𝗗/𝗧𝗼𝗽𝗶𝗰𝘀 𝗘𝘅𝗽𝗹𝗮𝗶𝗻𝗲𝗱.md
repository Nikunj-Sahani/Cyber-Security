- [ ] **Ethical Hacking:**
   > - Authorized testing of systems by simulating real attacks to identify and fix security vulnerabilities before malicious hackers exploit them.

- [ ] **Cyber Security:**
   > - The practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from cyber threats, attacks, and unauthorized access.

- [X] **Difference between Cyber security and Ethical Hacking**
   > - **Cyber Security:** Protects systems, networks, and data from attacks (defensive approach).
   > - **Ethical Hacking:** Actively tests systems by simulating attacks to find vulnerabilities (offensive approach).

- [ ] **Types of ethical hackers**

  > - **White Hat –** Authorized, ethical security professionals
  > - **Black Hat –** Malicious, illegal hackers
  > - **Gray Hat –** Act without permission but no harmful intent
  > - **Script Kiddies –** Use pre-made tools with limited knowledge
  > - **Hacktivists –** Hack for political or social causes
  > - **State-Sponsored Hackers –** Work for governments

- [ ] **Rules for Ethical Hacking:**

  > - **Permission –** Always have written authorization
  > - **Scope –** Stay within defined targets
  > - **No Damage –** Avoid data loss or system disruption
  > - **Confidentiality –** Keep findings private
  > - **Report –** Disclose vulnerabilities responsibly

- **Legality:**
Ethical hacking is legal only with proper permission and within scope; without it, it is treated as illegal hacking.
